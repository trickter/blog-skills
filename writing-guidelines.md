# Writing Guidelines

Use these guidelines when planning, drafting, and revising the blog post.

## Audience

Default audience:

- Technical readers
- Builders, engineers, or technically curious operators
- Readers who prefer judgment and synthesis over basic explanation

Assume the reader wants signal fast.

## Title Rules

Good titles in this workflow usually have these traits:

- A clear viewpoint, not just a topic label
- Tension, contrast, or an unexpected conclusion
- Enough specificity to feel worth clicking
- Short enough to scan, strong enough to remember

Prefer patterns like:

- `X 很热，但真正值得关注的是 Y`
- `关于 X，大多数人看错了这 1 点`
- `X 不是重点，真正的变量是 Y`
- `X 里，80% 是噪音：真正重要的是 ...`

Avoid:

- Generic how-to titles with no angle
- Empty hype words
- Titles that promise certainty without evidence

## Opening Rules

The opening must earn attention immediately.

In the first 2 to 4 sentences, do at least 2 of these:

- Tie the article to something current
- State the article's core judgment
- Create tension or contrast
- Tell the reader what they will understand differently

Preferred opening shape:

1. Current trigger
2. Direct judgment
3. Why most people get it wrong or miss the real issue

Avoid long background ramps.

## Body Structure

Use structure that supports a real argument:

1. State the claim
2. Show the evidence or reasoning
3. Address the weak spot, counterpoint, or hidden assumption
4. Return to practical implications

Strong sections usually do one job each:

- Explain why something matters
- Separate signal from noise
- Offer a decision framework
- Convert opinion into practice

## Tone

Write in Chinese that feels:

- Direct
- Calm
- Opinionated
- Grounded in reasoning

Do not sound like:

- A neutral news recap
- A marketing landing page
- A motivational speech

## Evidence And Claims

When using fresh facts, dates, rankings, or market observations:

- Base them on the research gathered earlier
- Prefer current and attributable information
- If evidence is partial, say so
- If a number is uncertain, avoid fake precision

It is acceptable to make a strong argument. It is not acceptable to fabricate support.

## Ending

The ending should not merely repeat the intro.

Prefer one of these ending moves:

- A sharper restatement of the article's real point
- A practical decision rule
- A final question that reframes the trend

The reader should leave with a decision lens, not just a summary.

## Final Self-Check

Before finalizing, verify:

- The title has a real angle
- The opening becomes interesting within a few sentences
- The article says something non-obvious
- The article is not just retelling source material
- Claims are as strong as the evidence allows
- The ending leaves the reader with a useful frame
